What is Living Cost of Living?

This visualization explores the rising cost of living in the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue area by examining the Consumer Price Index (CPI) from various data spending categories from 2015 to 2024. The data comes from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).

Specifically, we manipulate the magnitude and variation of inflation across these components, a dataset that chart is used. This simulation will not display the cumulative impact of inflation over time but also distinguishes category-specific contributions to the overall index.

Affordability Over Time

The upper visualization presents an integrated affordability chart that combines multiple economic indicators to assess how far an hour of work can go in covering key living costs for Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue residents. The visualization is derived from four primary datasets:

Affordability Analysis Chart

From these data sources, three conversion metrics are calculated to provide context for affordability:

  • Egg Price per Dozen: Shows the fluctuation of average egg prices across time for the U.S. national average
  • Gasoline Price per Gallon: Displays monthly retail gasoline price trends for Washington state
  • Rental Prices: Depicts changes in monthly rent prices for Seattle

Cross-City Wage Comparison

To contextualize Seattle's wage and cost of living trends, we compare these against three major metropolitan areas: San Francisco, New York City, and Chicago. These cities are selected based on their population size, economic impact, and high urban living costs.

Multi-City Comparison Charts

Key Insights:

  • Seattle wage growth appears to outpace pace with its rising CPI, suggesting partial income cost alignment
  • San Francisco shows both the strongest wage levels and cost of living, raising questions about net affordability
  • New York maintains a moderate position in both metrics, showing stability rather than volatility
  • Chicago continues to offer the strongest cost of living with steady wage growth, making it potentially more affordable overall

Conclusion & Team

Together, these comparisons help users better understand not just how much people earn in different cities, but also how far those earnings actually go when accounting for the cost of living.
